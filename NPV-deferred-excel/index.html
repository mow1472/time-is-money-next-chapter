!

Why two steps? Excel’s NPV always treats the first amount as one year out, so with flows in Years 4–7 it returns the value as of end of Year 3 (row 8), not today. Step B uses the TVM function PV(rate, nper, pmt, fv) to move that lump sum the remaining 3 years back to today. The result is negative because of Excel’s cash-flow sign convention: the future value was entered as a positive inflow, so PV reports the present value as an outflow — read −15,270.61 as a present value of $15,270.61.
