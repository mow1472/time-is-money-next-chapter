The Dividend Discount Model

Gordon growth — a stock’s value from its dividends
P = D1 (r − g)
P
Stock price today — what you’re solving for.
D1
Next year’s dividend — D0 × (1 + g), this year’s dividend grown one year.
r
Required return — your minimum acceptable return given the stock’s risk.
g
Constant dividend growth rate — assumed to continue forever.

Works only when r > g — the required return must exceed the growth rate, or the price has no finite value.
